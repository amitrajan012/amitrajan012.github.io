8.2 Bagging, Random Forests, Boosting 8.2.1 Bagging The decision trees discussed above suffers from a problem of high variance. Bootstrap aggregation or bagging is a procedure that reduces the variance of a statistical learning method. Give a set of $n$ independent observation sets $Z_1, Z_2, …, Z_n$, each with variance $\sigma^2$, the variance of the mean $\bar{Z}$ is given by $\sigma^2/n$, i.e. averaging a set of observations reduces variance. Hence, a natural way to reduce the variance of a statistical model is to take many training samples from the population, fit individual models on them, and give the average of them as the final model.
