Confidence Intervals

Confidence Intervals for Proportions : Let $X$ be the number of successes in $n$ independent Bernoulli trials with success probability $p$, where the number of trials $n$ is large enough, such that $X \sim Bin(n, p)$. Then the $100(1 - \alpha) \%$ confidence interval for $p$ is: $$\widehat{p} \pm z_{\alpha/2}\sqrt{\frac{\widehat{p}(1-\widehat{p})}{n}}$$ where $\widehat{p}$ is the sample proportion and can be estimated as $\frac{X}{n}$. It should be noted that the quantity under the square root is the sample variance.
